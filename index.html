Website Principles

(oh yeah, and new menus...)

Put users first

  • Understand our usersâ€™ needs
  • Provide the best possible user experience by focusing on those needs
  • Acknowledge that we are different from our users

Be credible and trustworthy

  • Verify information
  • Remove outdated or inaccurate content
  • Present relevant content at point of need
  • Maintain accountability for content ownership

Keep it simple and clear

  • Make thiings intuitive
  • Remove superfluous information and distractions
  • Use concise, consistent language
  • Simplify

Provide clear hierarchy and visual cues

  • Give the most crucial elements the greatest prominence
  • Optimize pages to support the most frequent and important tasks
  • Group related content together
  • Provide signs or cues for wayfinding

Eliminate library jargon

  • Use language that is natural and clear to our users
  • Do not require users to decipher (or even be aware of) our internal terminology

Design for a diverse audience using a wide range of devices

  • Make content usable to as large an audience as possible
  • Make content usable on as many devices as possible
  • Adhere to accessibility guidelines
  • Embrace responsive design and progressive enhancement

Innovate and evolve

  • Explore new ways to meet users' current needs
  • Anticipate future needs
  • Take risks...
  • ...make small changes often...
  • ...and assess outcomes
